Meet Your Trainer
"I know how stressful the OSCE feels as an international nurse. I've dedicated my career to making sure you pass with confidence."

Shalu Kumar is a highly experienced nurse trainer based in Cambridge, specialising in helping international nurses prepare for the NMC OSCE exam. With over 20 years of experience across the NHS and private healthcare sector, she combines deep clinical expertise with supportive, personalised teaching tailored to every nurse's individual needs.

Before founding ONTC Cambridge, Shalu led one of the UK's largest OSCE training programmes at Cambridge University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, helping overseas nurses achieve strong pass rates and excellent course feedback. Originally trained in India before relocating to the United Kingdom in 1998, she has held senior roles across both NHS and private healthcare, including at Spire Healthcare, and is dedicated to helping nurses build confidence and succeed in their OSCE journey.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know

The OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) is a mandatory skills assessment for overseas nurses registering with the NMC to work in the UK. It tests clinical competencies across multiple stations. Specialist training dramatically increases your pass rate — our students achieve 95%+ success on their first attempt.

Training duration depends on your chosen course, time required for revision keeping in mind your personal and work commitments. We would generally recommend a period of 10 weeks from start of training to taking your OSCE exam.

We offer both options. The Virtual course is fully online and includes an online mock exam. Our blended course starts with online lessons to cover the theory part and then you attend 5 practice days to do hands-on practise using real clinical equipment in exam-simulated conditions. Tailored 1:1 training can be done virtually or in-person as per your needs.

Absolutely. We have successfully trained nurses from the UAE, Nigeria, Ghana, India, Philippines, Nepal, Romania, Kenya, Pakistan, US, New Zealand, Australia, Sri Lanka, Hong Kong and many more. The OSCE is the same for all internationally educated nurses applying to register with the NMC, and our training is designed specifically for this audience.

We are hugely proud of our above 95% pass rate at first attempt which means most of our nurses pass first time. In the rare event that a nurse needs to resit, we work closely with them to identify what went wrong and prepare specifically for the resit.

Practical sessions use real clinical equipment to help nurses prepare for real OSCE exam conditions.
